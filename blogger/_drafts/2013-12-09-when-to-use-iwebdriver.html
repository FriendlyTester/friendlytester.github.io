--- layout: post title: When To Use IWebDriver date: '2013-12-09T14:25:00.000Z' author: Richard Bradshaw tags: modified_time: '2013-12-09T14:25:07.866Z' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-8318661666872903125.post-6646411816105987559 blogger_orig_url: http://www.thefriendlytester.co.uk/2013/12/when-to-use-iwebdriver.html --- I tend to use IWebDriver for most of my PO's this is because the CORE functionality of WebDriver will always be available on supported drivers.

This will stop you use methods specific to a customer browser such as RemoteWebDriver or Chrome/Firefox etc.

However if you do have something specific to a browser you can still use this in your PO by casting the IWebDriver instance to the required driver, allowing you to use those methods.

But if you write all your POs against a certain driver you may run into issues when trying to use other drivers, or that specific driver changes its naming conventioned or approach
